1.1 Basic Pre-Flight

TOC 1.1 Basic Pre-Flight CompletedComments
No. Basic Operations Pre-Flight Checklist
1 Ensure flight will be 5.6 km (3 NM) away from airports or military aerodromes, and 1.9 km (1 NM) away from heliports.
2 Ensure flight will not be in Controlled Airspace or Restricted Airspace.
3 Locate other aerodromes in flight area and ensure flight stays out of established flight patterns of those aerodromes.
4 Ensure no impacting temporary aviation restrictions are in effect.
5 Check visually and audibly for nearby low-altitude air operations such as helicopters, seaplanes, hot air balloons, and ultra-light aircraft.
6 Take note of potential obstacles such as buildings, wires, trees, cell towers, wind turbines.
7 Check for and plan to stay clear of land-based airspace restrictions, including police activities, forest fires, or other natural disasters.
8 Check suitability of current weather and weather forecast for the duration of the operation.
9 Check that the Kp index is suitable for the flight (ideally less than 4).
10 Check for and avoid potential dust or dirt clouds.
11 Determine the maximum safe range for the operation, based upon site details, current weather conditions, and battery charge.
12 Ensure bystanders are at least 30m away from aircraft at all times.
13 Ensure crew and other involved parties are aware of the flight and stand clear.
14 Stay at least 30m away from advertised events such as parades, sporting events, or outdoor concerts.
15 Check that drone is registered and registration number is on drone.
16 Check for damage, cracks, or other signs of potential failure.
17 Check propellers for cracks, deformation, or other wear.
18 Ensure drone and control unit batteries are sufficiently charged for the operation.
19 Check that propellers, propeller guards, and other removable items are secure, and items such as gimbal guards and lens covers are removed.
20 Ensure micro-SD card is installed for camera recording.
21 Ensure launch site is suitable for take-off (level, clear of debris, etc.).
22 Confirm that pilot is certified for at least Basic Operations.
23 Confirm that all flight crew members are fit to perform: No alcohol within 12 hours, no cannabis for 28 days, no impairing drugs, not fatigued.
24 Ensure all flight crew members understand their roles, communication methods, emergency procedures, and emergency equipment.
25 Ensure any other people involved in the operation stay clear during takeoff/landing and do not interfere.
26 Confirm crew is aware of take-off, landing and back-up landing plan.
27 Ensure any special attributes of the flight are addressed (e.g., FPV, night flying).
28 Ensure flight log is available.

1.2 Advanced Pre-Flight

TOC 1.2 Advanced Pre-Flight CompletedComments
No. Advanced Operations Pre-Flight Checklist
1 If flight will be nearer than 5.6 km (3 NM) away from airports, or 1.9 km from heliports, contact the operator and secure permission.
2 If flight will be in Controlled or Restricted Airspace, confirm drone is accepted and obtain NAV CANADA RPAS Flight Authorization.
3 If flight is near a military aerodrome, ensure an SFOC has been approved and permission secured.
4 Locate nearby aerodromes and stay out of their flight patterns.
5 Check for temporary aviation restrictions (NOTAMs).
6 Visually and audibly check for low altitude aircraft.
7 Note potential obstacles: buildings, wires, trees, etc.
8 Avoid land-based airspace restrictions (police activity, fires, disasters).
9 Check current and forecasted weather.
10 Ensure Kp index is suitable (less than 4).
11 Avoid dust or dirt clouds.
12 Determine safe flight range based on weather and battery.
13 If flying between 5m and 30m from bystanders, drone must be approved for Near People.
14 If flying under 5m from bystanders, drone must be approved for Over People.
15 Ensure all involved parties are aware and stay clear.
16 If flying at an advertised event, an SFOC is required.
17 Drone must be registered and labeled.
18 Ensure drone is approved for controlled airspace or people operations.
19 Check for physical damage or wear.
20 Inspect propellers for issues.
21 Verify battery levels are sufficient.
22 Check all attachments are secure and camera guards removed.
23 Confirm SD card is installed.
24 Launch site must be level and clear.
25 Pilot must be certified for Advanced Operations or under supervision.
26 Crew must be fit (no alcohol in 12h, no cannabis in 28d, not fatigued).
27 Crew must understand roles, communication, and emergency procedures.
28 All others must stay clear and not interfere.
29 Ensure crew knows takeoff, landing, and backup plans.
30 Address any special flight attributes (e.g., night flying, FPV).
31 Ensure flight log is available.

1.3 Micro Pre-Flight

TOC 1.3 Micro Pre-Flight CompletedComments
No. Micro Drones Pre-Flight Checklist
1 If flying near airports or heliports, fly below 30m AGL when possible.
2 If flying in Controlled Airspace, fly below 30m AGL.
3 If near military aerodrome, fly carefully and ideally below 30m AGL.
4 If in CYR Class F Zone, obtain permission from controlling agency.
5 Avoid established aerodrome patterns.
6 Check for temporary flight restrictions (NOTAMs, apps).
7 Visually and audibly monitor for other aircraft.
8 Watch for obstacles: buildings, wires, trees, etc.
9 Avoid restricted zones (e.g., emergencies, wildfires).
10 Get landowner permission for takeoff/landing in parks.
11 Check weather and forecast.
12 Kp index should be under 4.
13 Avoid dusty environments.
14 Determine safe range based on site/weather/battery.
15 Keep bystanders clear during takeoff and landing.
16 Do not fly over crowds.
17 Ensure crew and involved parties are briefed.
18 Check for physical damage.
19 Inspect propellers.
20 Verify full battery charge.
21 Ensure attachments are secure and gimbal guards removed.
22 Install micro-SD card if recording.
23 Ensure clear, level launch site.
24 Crew must be unimpaired and rested.
25 Brief all crew on flight plan.

2.0 Takeoff Procedures

TOC 2.0 Takeoff Procedures CompletedComments
No. Takeoff Procedures Checklist
1 Confirm completion of pre-flight checklist
2 Record beginning of flight log information
3 Assemble control unit, display, and related gear
4 Prepare drone for launch
5 Activate control unit and display device
6 Launch drone control app on phone or control
7 Activate drone
8 Initiate control unit and drone communication connection, and confirm proper linkage
9 Ensure all control switches are correctly set for operation
10 Check that firmware load for entire system are up to date and consistent
11 Check that compass, IMU, and gimbal calibration are ok
12 Ensure Return-to-Home parameters are correct for current operation
13 Ensure sufficient GPS satellites are connected (minimum 7)
14 Perform final weather check
15 Signal to crew and bystanders to stand clear for take-off
16 Start camera recording
17 Announce take-off and stand clear
18 Activate take-off controls
19 Allow drone to hover at low altitude to confirm stability
20 Test controls to ensure full directional control

3.0 Landing Procedures

TOC 3.0 Landing Procedures CompletedComments
No. Landing Procedures
1 Alert crew and bystanders of approaching aircraft
2 Ensure landing site is clear of debris and is suitable for landing
3 Rotate drone to face away before final approach (natural control)
4 Ensure all crew and people are clear of landing site
5 Ensure all bystanders are 30m away from landing site
6 Land drone
7 Turn off motors and camera
8 Retrieve drone and inspect for damage
9 Disassemble as required, apply gimbal guard and lens cover, stow gear
10 Record flight in flight log

4.1 General Emergencies

TOC 4.1 General Emergencies CompletedComments
No. General Emergency Check List
1 Administer first aid as appropriate
2 Call 911 if required
3 Stop flight operation
4 Report serious injuries to Transportation Safety Board (1-800-387-3557)
5 Attempt to reconnect to drone (antennae, moving closer, restarting controller/app)
6 Record direction, speed, altitude, battery life of drone
7 Use Emergency button in app to notify aerodromes/control towers
8 Call Aviation Emergency Number: 1-877-992-6853 if threat to public safety
9 Warn people in potential crash zone if possible
10 Recover drone and inspect
11 Investigate and log cause of incident
12 Power cycle control station and reconnect to drone
13 Check and log equipment malfunctions in maintenance log

4.2 Horizontal Fly Away

TOC 4.2 Horizontal Fly Away CompletedComments
No. Horizontal Flyaway
1 Attempt to reconnect to aircraft (antennae, controller, app)
2 Note Emergency button on Flight Timer screen of Drone Pilot Canada app
3 Estimate RPA altitude, speed, direction, battery life
4 Call aerodromes in direction of travel (nearest first)
5 Call FIR Area Control Centre Emergency Number
6 If threat is imminent, call Aviation Emergency Number: 1-877-992-6853
7 Use communication script when contacting aerodromes/emergency contacts

4.3 Vertical Fly Away

TOC 4.3 Vertical Fly Away CompletedComments
No. Vertical Flyaway
1 Attempt to reconnect to aircraft (antennae, controller, app)
2 Note Emergency button in Drone Pilot Canada app
3 Call FIR Area Control Centre Emergency Number
4 If FIR unreachable, call nearest airport with control tower
5 If threat is imminent, call Aviation Emergency Number: 1-877-992-6853
6 Use communication script to provide drone info (altitude, speed, battery, direction)

5.1 Basic Rules

TOC 5.1 Basic Rules CompletedComments
No. Basic Flight Rules
1 Drone must be < 25 kg, registered, and labeled
2 Pilot must pass Basic exam and be 14+ or supervised
3 Maintain VLOS; FPV only with VO; night flight needs lights
4 Stay 5.6 km from airports, 1.9 km from heliports, avoid patterns
5 Avoid controlled/restricted airspace
6 Stay 30m from bystanders; no overflight of people/events
7 Max altitude: 122m or 30m above structures within 61m

5.2 Advanced Rules

TOC 5.2 Advanced Rules CompletedComments
No. Advanced Flight Rules
1 Drone must be <25 kg, registered, and labeled
2 Pilot must pass Advanced exam and be 16+ or supervised
3 Maintain VLOS; FPV only with VO; night flight needs lights
4 Can fly near airports/heliports with permission; DND requires SFOC
5 May fly in controlled/restricted airspace with approval and approved drone
6 May fly near/over people if using approved drone
7 No flying at advertised events; same altitude rules apply

6.0 SFOC Requirement

TOC 6.0 SFOC Requirement CompletedComments
No. Special Flight Operations Certificate
1 Drone weighs >25 kg
2 Flying beyond VLOS
3 Pilot is not Canadian citizen or permanent resident
4 Flying above max altitude without ATC approval
5 Controlling >5 RPAs from one station
6 Flying at special/advertised event
7 Carrying hazardous payload
8 Flying within 3 NM of military aerodrome
9 Minister-designated operations

7.0 Visual Observer Duties

TOC 7.0 Visual Observer Duties CompletedComments
No. Visual Observer Duties
1 Assist Pilot In Command (PIC) by maintaining VLOS and detecting hazards
2 Scan for aircraft, obstacles, people, and environmental threats
3 Inform PIC if VLOS is lost or hazards arise
4 Pass VLOS duties if temporarily distracted
5 Know mission and hazard limits (e.g. wind, weather, people)
6 Comply with PIC instructions; communicate effectively
7 Remain sober, rested, and unimpaired
8 Use unaided vision only during flight
9 Cannot drive or operate vehicles while on VO duty
10 Be familiar with RPAS regulations and aircraft limits
11 May support pre-flight, checklist, and ground crew duties
